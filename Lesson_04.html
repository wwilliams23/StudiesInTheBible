The Patriarchs

Lesson 4

In our last lesson we traced the history of man through the Bible from the creation to the flood. We also studied the events immediately following the flood. In this lesson we shall study the lives of the patriarchs and the story of how their descendants became a great people while slaves in Egypt. The period from the creation to the giving of the law of Moses is called the Patriarchal Age because the patriarchs were the spiritual leaders of this dispensation or period.

The word "patriarch" means "father." The three great patriarchs -- Abraham, Isaac, and Jacob -- are so-called because they were the "fathers" of the Hebrew people whom we know as the childern of Israel or Jews.

Abraham

A direct descendant of Shem, one of Noah's sons, Abraham was born in Ur of the Chaldees in the southern part of the presend day country of Iraq. God saw in Abraham (then known as Abram) a good man. Instructing him to leave his people to go into a land He would show him, God made several promises to him (Gen. 12:1-7). (1) He would make of him a great Nation. Abraham was to become the father of the Israelites. (2) He would make his Name great. Today he is honored the world over by Jews and Christians. (3) He would give him a Land. What Abraham knew as Canaan and we as Israel, was to become the home of the children of Israel. (4) He would bless those who blessed Abraham and curse those who cursed him. The fate of Israel's neighboring nations was to be determined by their attitude toward God's chosen people. (5) In him all the nations of the earth would be Blessed. This was fulfilled in Jesus Christ, Abraham's descendant, who would bless the world in making salvation possible for all mankind. The chief reason the people of Israel were chosen by God was that it was through them that the Savior of the world would come!

In obedience to the divine call Abraham went to Canaan. Genesis 12-25 relates many events from his time in that land. His nephew Lot moved to the wicked city of Sodom. Abraham asked God to spare the city for Lot's sake, but so few good people were found that God burned it aloong with the city of Gomorrah. Lot was delivered, but his wife in disobedience to God looked back and was turned to a piller of salt. What a lesson for us that we must always obey God!

Abraham's oldest son was Ishmael, whose mother was Hagar, the handmaid of Sarah, Abraham's wife. Isaac, Sarah's son, was born when Abraham was 100 years old and Sarah 90. Ishmael became the father of the Arabs, while God fulfilled his promise to Abraham through Isaac and his descendants, Perhaps no greater example of faith can be found than when Abraham offered Isaac as a sacrifice in response to God's command. When the Lord saw thae faith of Abraham, He stayed his hand and saved Isaac's life. The story is in Genesis 22. At age 175 Abraham died and was buried in the cave of Machpelah beside Sarah.

Isaac

The second of the patriarchs was Isaac who lived to be 180. To him God repeated His promise to Abraham that in his seed all nations would be blessed. Isaac married his cousin, Rebekah, and to her were born twins, Jacob and Esau. It was through the younger of these, Jacob, that God fulfilled His promise.

Jacob

The third patriarch, Jacob, was renamed Israel by God. The expression "Children of Israel" refers to Jacob's descendants. From Esau Jacob bought his birthright for a mess of pottarge (Gen.25) He also deceived his father to get the blessing belonging to his brother (Gen. 27). Having incurred Esau's ire, he fled to Haran where he met Rachel for whom he worked seven years to have her as his wife. Her father decieved him and gave him Leah, Rachel's older sister, instead. So Jacob married both women and worked another seven years for Rachel (Gen. 28, 29). Jacob had twelve sons and a daughter, Dinah. The sons became the fathers of the twelve tribes of Israel.

The story of Jocab's sons is best told in the life of Joseph, his favorite. He is one of the few important Bible characters of whom nothing evil is said. His brother became jealous because of Jacob's favoritism and sold Joseph into slavery, telling their father that Joseph had been killed. Through the course of divine providence Joseph became a servant to Potiphar, captain of the Egyptian Pharaoh's guard. Due to no fault of his he was cast into prison. Later, when he was able to interpret the king's dream forcasting seven years of plenty to be followed by seven years of famine, he was elevated to a position second only to the king. He managed the royal affairs well, saving grain during the years of plenty for use during the famine that followed. The famine caused Jacob to send ten of his sons to Egypt to buy grain where for the first time in many years they met the brother whom they had sold into slavery. The story of the reunion of the brothers is one of the most touching that can be read. All was forgiven and Joseph instructed them to bring their father and their families into Egypt. Read this beautiful story in Genesis 42-46.

The Egyptian Bondage

After the death of Jacob and Joseph, the children of Israel so multiplied that when they left Egypt 430 years later (Exodus 12:40) they had increased from 70 to 600,000 men plus children. In the meantime, another Pharaoh "who knew not Joseph" came to the throne. Instead of granting them a favored position, he made them slaves. God raised a deliverer in the person of Moses. The story of Moses is a wonderful one - how he was placed in an ark on the river as a baby because his parents feared he would be killed, how he was discovered by the king's daugther and raised as a prince, how he gave up his royaol home and accepted the leadership of his people to deliver them from bondage.

Moses and his brother, Aaron, who became his spokesman, appeared before Pharaoh to try to persuade him to allow the Israelites to leave Egypt. The king refused and only after God had brought ten plagues on the land did he relent to allow them to depart. The last plague was the most terrible of all as the firstborn of each Egyptian family died, even Pharaoh's oldest child. For the complete story of the patriarchs read Genesis 12 -- Exodus 12. The story of how Israel left Egypt and for forty years wandered in the wilderness will be told next.
